### Approach A: "British Heritage Transport Authority"
**Design Movement:** British Institutional Modernism — think the gravitas of National Rail branding crossed with the authority of a government transport body.

**Core Principles:**
1. Authoritative asymmetry — heavy left-anchored content blocks with open right-side breathing room
2. Typographic hierarchy as the primary visual language
3. Structural rigidity with warm orange accents as punctuation marks
4. Trust signals embedded architecturally (not as afterthoughts)

**Color Philosophy:** Deep navy (#0A1628) as the dominant authority colour — commanding and trustworthy. Crisp white (#FFFFFF) for content areas. Burnt orange (#E8610A) as the action/energy colour, used sparingly for CTAs, dividers, and key stats. A warm mid-grey (#F5F4F2) for section backgrounds to break monotony without losing professionalism.

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial layout. Hero section uses a 60/40 split — left side carries bold typographic statement, right side carries the coach image with an angled orange accent stripe. Sections alternate between full-bleed navy and white/grey backgrounds.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Diagonal orange stripe dividers between sections (clip-path cuts)
2. Bold oversized section numbers (01, 02, 03) in very light navy as background watermarks
3. Thick left-border accent lines in orange on key callout boxes

**Interaction Philosophy:** Deliberate and confident. Hover states shift elements slightly right with an orange underline reveal. CTAs use a fill-from-left animation on hover.

**Animation:** Entrance animations are staggered fade-up (30ms stagger). Hero text uses a word-by-word reveal. Counter animations for stats (years experience, vehicles, bookings). Scroll-triggered section reveals.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display Bold — authority and heritage
- Body: Source Sans 3 — clean, legible, modern
- Accent labels: Barlow Condensed Semibold — industrial, transport-feel

### Approach B: "Modern Mobility Premium" ← SELECTED
**Design Movement:** Contemporary British Corporate — the visual language of premium logistics brands like DHL Express or National Express's premium tier, but warmer and more approachable.

**Core Principles:**
1. Confident grid with deliberate white space — nothing feels cramped
2. Photography-forward — coaches and journeys are the heroes
3. Orange as energy, navy as trust, white as clarity
4. Micro-interactions that reward exploration

**Color Philosophy:** Rich navy (#0D1F3C) anchors the brand — deep, established, trustworthy. Vibrant orange (#F97316) brings energy and urgency to CTAs and highlights. White (#FFFFFF) provides the clean canvas. Subtle warm grey (#F8F7F5) for alternating sections. A light navy tint (#EEF2F8) for card backgrounds.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-width hero with a floating quote form card overlapping the fold. Services presented in an asymmetric masonry-style grid. Fleet section uses horizontal scroll cards. Testimonials in a staggered two-column layout.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Orange accent bars — thin 4px horizontal lines above section headings
2. Coach silhouette SVG watermarks in very light navy behind section backgrounds
3. Pill-shaped service tags in orange on service cards

**Interaction Philosophy:** Smooth and premium. Quote form has live passenger count slider. Service cards lift with a subtle shadow on hover. Navigation has an underline-slide indicator.

**Animation:** Smooth scroll-triggered fade-up with slight Y-translate (20px → 0). Hero has a subtle Ken Burns effect on the background image. Stats counter on scroll. Form fields animate in sequentially on focus.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Raleway ExtraBold — strong, modern, distinctive
- Body: Nunito Sans — friendly yet professional
- UI labels: DM Sans Medium — clean interface text

### Approach C: "Road Atlas Editorial"
**Design Movement:** Transport Editorial — inspired by vintage AA road maps and modern transport journalism. Structured like a broadsheet but with bold digital energy.

**Core Principles:**
1. Grid-based but broken intentionally — one element always bleeds or overlaps
2. Map/route visual metaphors throughout
3. Strong typographic contrast between display and body
4. Data-driven trust (numbers, stats, coverage maps)

**Color Philosophy:** Deep navy (#1A2744) as primary. Warm orange (#F4811F) as accent. Cream (#FAF8F3) as background — warmer than white, feels established. Dark charcoal (#2D2D2D) for body text.

**Layout Paradigm:** Newspaper-style multi-column layouts for content sections. Hero uses a full-bleed map graphic with route lines overlaid. Coverage section uses an actual interactive UK map.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Route line graphics connecting service locations
2. Vintage-style destination badges/stamps
3. Bold pull-quotes styled as road signs

**Interaction Philosophy:** Exploratory — users discover content by navigating like a map. Hover states reveal additional route info.

**Animation:** Route lines draw themselves on scroll. Location pins drop in sequence. Page transitions use a horizontal slide suggesting travel.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Bebas Neue — bold, directional, transport-feel
- Body: Lato — clean and widely trusted
- Accent: Oswald — for stats and callouts

## Selected Approach: **B — Modern Mobility Premium**

Rich navy (#0D1F3C) + vibrant orange (#F97316) + white. Raleway ExtraBold display, Nunito Sans body. Photography-forward with floating quote form, asymmetric service grid, and smooth scroll animations.
